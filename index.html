Hi, this is Hui Yang (杨辉)

PhD Student, The George Washington University

I study various X-ray and gamma-ray sources, such as neutron stars, black holes, accreting binaries.
I am curious about the radiation mechanisms underlying high-energy astrophysical phenomena, such as the interplay between inflows (e.g., accretion) and outflows (e.g., AGN jets, pulsar wind nebulae).
My research involves the utilization of multiwavelength data and methods like machine learning classifications.

Research Highlight

We discovered and studied a new gamma-ray-emitting narrow-line Seyfert 1 (NLS1) galaxy, SDSS J211852.96−073227.5 (hereafter J2118) Yang et al. 2018, among only a handful of gamma-ray NLS1s so far. This source is particularly interesting as it exhibits variability (see first plot below for the GeV light curve of J2118) across the wavelengths and the host galaxy morphology (see second plot below of the r band image of J2118) suggests an interacting system.

I classified a large fraction (21%) of X-ray sources from Chandra Source Catalog version 2.0 using a multiwavelength machine learning pipeline (MUWCLASS; Yang et al. 2022). The data and software used in this paper are available at this URL and also on Vizier. The interactive training dataset visualization is available at this URL).
